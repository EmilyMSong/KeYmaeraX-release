solve replaces a (solvable) differential equation x'=f(x) by an assignment x:=x(t) to its solution x(t) at time t along with a quantifier for time t≥0. Evolution domain constraints q(x) are handled correspondingly to check that q(x(s)) was true at all intermediate times 0≤s≤t.

Invariant techniques are needed for differential equations that have no solutions in (polynomial) real arithmetic. Reasoning by invariants is also often faster.

See also

  • dC differential cuts: to first prove another property of the differential equation and then assume it during the dynamics.
  • dI differential invariants: to prove a property that remains true locally in the direction of the dynamics.
  • dW differential weakening: to prove a property that directly follows from the evolution domain constraint.
  • dG differential ghosts: to add a fresh variable with a new differential equation into the dynamics.
