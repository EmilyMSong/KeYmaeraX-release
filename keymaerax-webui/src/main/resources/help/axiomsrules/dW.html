dW differential weakening tries proving the postcondition P of a differential equation system [{x′=f(x) & Q}]P from its evolution domain constraint Q by reducing it to Q→P. If this succeeds, the property is independent of x′=f(x).

See also

  • dI Differential invariant: to prove the formula R is an invariant of the differential equation system.
  • dC differential cuts: to augment the evolution domain with stronger provable invariants such that dW will later succeed.
